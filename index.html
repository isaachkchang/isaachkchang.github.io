About me

Hi! My name Isaac Chang, and I am a Neuroscience PhD student
at the Univeristy of California, San Francisco

I grew up in Hong Kong and received my Bachelor’s degree in Biochemistry and Cell Biology at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST). My work at HKUST mainly focuses on identifying pathways to enhance axon regeneration after spinal cord injury. During my junior year, I went to Northwestern for a research internship and worked with Professor Savio Chan to characterize different GPe neuron subtypes and survey their electrophysiological properties, projection patterns and behavior roles. Right now, I am interested in exploring the cellular and molecular makeup of the basal ganglia circuit, and investigate the disease alterations that occur in various movement disorders such as Parkinson's Disease.
